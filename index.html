Sequences of Symmetries

Take the sequence of values \(m\) such that, given \( a_{k} =\sum _{n=0}^{k}( -1)^{\lfloor ns\rfloor }\), $$a_{k} =a_{2m-k}$$ For \(k=0,1,\dotsc , m\). Alternatively, write \( k=m-j\) for \(j=0,1,\dotsc m\). Then (1) can also be written as: $$a_{m-j} =a_{m+j}$$ We call these \( m_{k}( s)\) the symmetries of \(\displaystyle s\). In the following table, \(q_{k} =m_{k+1} -m_{k} ,\ r_{k} =q_{k+1} -q_{k}\) are the first and second differences, respectively, and \( t_{k} =\frac{r_{k}}{q_{k}}\) are the difference quotients. In the table we consider the limits of subsequences of \(t_{k}\) for \(k\) which are congruent modulo some \(\tau(p)\).
