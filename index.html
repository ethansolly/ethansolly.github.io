Math

Let \(s\) be an irrational real number. Take the sequence of values \(m\) such that, given \( a_{k} =\sum _{n=0}^{k}( -1)^{\lfloor ns\rfloor }\), $$a_{k} =a_{2m-k}$$ For \(k=0,1,\dotsc , m\). Alternatively, write \( k=m-j\) for \(j=0,1,\dotsc m\). Then (1) can also be written as: $$a_{m-j} =a_{m+j}$$ We call these \( m_{k}( s)\) the symmetries of \(\displaystyle s\). In the following table, \(q_{k} =m_{k+1} -m_{k} ,\ r_{k} =q_{k+1} -q_{k}\) are the first and second differences, respectively, and \( t_{k} =\frac{r_{k}}{q_{k}}\) are the difference quotients. In the table we consider the limits of subsequences of \(t_{k}\) for \(k\) which are congruent modulo some \(\tau(p)\).

Let \( f( x) =\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^{x} 2^{-\lfloor x\log_{2}( 3/2)\rfloor }\).

Then:

  • \( a_0 = 1 \)
  • \( a_n \) is the smallest natural number such that \( f(a_n) < f(a_{n-1}) \)

Some values:

n 0 1 2 3 4 5
\(a_n\) 1 2 7 12 53 359

These correspond to \(a_n\)-ET (equal temperament) tunings that are each successively closer to a Pythagorean tuning.
